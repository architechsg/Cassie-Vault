# FAQ — Funding & Subsidies

**Q: What is SkillsFuture Credit and how do I use it?**
A: SkillsFuture Credit is a government scheme giving Singapore Citizens funds to offset course fees. Log in at myskillsfuture.gov.sg with Singpass and submit a claim using the course's TGS code **before** the course start date. See [[funding/skillsfuture-credit]].

**Q: Do I need the Singpass App?**
A: Yes, if you are claiming SSG funding. Download the Singpass App before class day — without it, attendance cannot be captured electronically and funding will not be approved. See [[concepts/singpass-attendance]].

**Q: How is GST calculated on subsidised fees?**
A: GST (9%) is calculated on the full pre-subsidy fee, not on what you pay. Your total = subsidised fee + (9% × full pre-GST fee). See [[funding/pricing-tiers]] for worked examples.

**Q: I'm 42 years old and a Singapore Citizen. Which price do I pay?**
A: You qualify for the MCES (40+) rate — the lowest individual price. This is typically the same as the MCES 21–39 rate or lower. Check the specific course page for the exact fee.

**Q: I'm a PR. Am I eligible for subsidies?**
A: Yes, Singapore PRs aged 21+ qualify for the PR subsidy (~30% off the full fee). You can also use SkillsFuture Credit if you are a Singapore Citizen (PRs are not eligible for SFC).

**Q: Can I use UTAP?**
A: If you are an NTUC union member, yes. UTAP provides up to 50% co-funding on your remaining out-of-pocket fees, capped at $250/year. Most WSQ courses are UTAP-eligible. See [[funding/utap-ntuc]].

**Q: My company wants to sponsor me. What funding is available?**
A: SME-sponsored employees can get up to 90% subsidy (ETSS). Companies also qualify for Absentee Payroll ($4.50/hr) and SkillsFuture Enterprise Credit. See [[funding/corporate-funding]].

**Q: What if I miss more than 25% of classes?**
A: You will not be eligible for SSG funding and may be charged the full course fee. See [[policies/attendance-requirements]].
