# Corporate & Employer Funding

## For Companies Sponsoring Employees

### Absentee Payroll (AP)
- **$4.50 per hour** per trainee while they are in training
- Capped at **$100,000 per enterprise per year**
- Available for **Singapore Citizens and PRs only**
- Compensates companies for the salary cost of sending employees to train

### Enhanced Training Support for SMEs (ETSS)
- Subsidies of up to **70%** of course fees for SME-sponsored employees on Coursemology courses. (The up-to-90% ETSS rate applies only to courses run by CET Centres, Autonomous Universities, Polytechnics, ITE and NIEC, which does not include Coursemology, an external training provider.)
- Under ETSS the 70% rate is **by citizenship only — no age bands**: every eligible Singapore Citizen (any age) and PR gets 70%.
- Available for **Singapore Citizens and PRs only**
- SME = registered in Singapore with no more than 200 employees, or annual sales turnover of no more than $100 million
- Employee must be on payroll and contributing CPF on the training day
- SME status is checked and declared on the EPJS portal (enterprisejobskills.gov.sg)

### Non-SME / MNC / other corporate entities
- These do **not** qualify for ETSS. Their sponsored employees fall under the **standard individual SSG tiers** (age + citizenship based): SC aged 21–39 = 50%, SC aged 40+ = 70% (MCES), PR aged 21+ = 50%. See [[funding/ssg-subsidies]].
- Absentee Payroll (Singapore Citizens and PRs only) still applies.

### SkillsFuture Enterprise Credit (SFEC)
- Enterprise credits for transformation-related training
- Applied at the enterprise level (not per-trainee)
- Contact Coursemology for details on eligible programmes

### Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS)
- Covers **20% of the remaining course fees** after SSG subsidies and SFEC have been applied
- Contact the corporate training consultant for eligibility and how to claim
