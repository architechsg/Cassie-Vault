# Pricing Tiers Explained

> **Note for Cassie:** Actual per-course fees are **not stored in this vault**. They are returned live from the `get_course_schedule` tool (`pricing.full`, `pricing.sc_pr`, `pricing.mces`). Use this page only to explain the pricing structure to visitors — never quote prices from this page directly.

## The Three Price Points
Every WSQ course has three published prices (all GST-inclusive):

| Label | Who Pays This | Subsidy Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Full Fee** | Foreigners, Singapore Citizens under 21, ineligible individuals | No subsidy |
| **PR Price** | Singapore PRs aged 21+ | ~30% SSG subsidy |
| **MCES Price** | Singapore Citizens aged 21–39 (and 40+ share same or lower price) | ~45–65% SSG subsidy |

> For many courses, SC 40+ pays the same MCES price as SC 21–39. For some courses, SC 40+ or SME-sponsored employees get a lower price.

## How GST Is Calculated
GST (9%) is charged on the **net course fee after any discount, but before subsidy**. A discount and a subsidy are different things and affect GST differently:

- A **discount** (e.g. a promotional or package discount) is applied first and **lowers the GST**, because GST is charged on the discounted (net) price.
- A **government subsidy** (SSG funding by age and citizenship) lowers the fee the student pays but does **NOT** lower the GST. GST stays based on the net fee.

**Formula:** Total payable = (net fee after discount − subsidy) + 9% GST on the net fee.

**Example A — no discount (Food Safety Level 1):**
- Full fee: $150
- GST: $150 × 9% = $13.50
- Full fee (incl. GST): **$163.50**
- PR subsidised fee: ~$75 + $13.50 GST = **$88.50**
- MCES (SC 40+) subsidised fee: ~$45 + $13.50 GST = **$58.50**

**Example B — with a $30 discount:**
- Full fee $150 − $30 discount = **net $120**
- GST: $120 × 9% = **$10.80** (lower, because the discount reduced the base)
- The subsidy by age/citizenship then applies to work out the student's course fee, with the $10.80 GST added on top.

(Exact fees always come live from the schedule tool — these examples just show the structure.)

## Why Does This Matter?
When a student uses SkillsFuture Credit to pay, the SFC covers the subsidised portion. The GST component must still be paid in cash. This is why SFC alone may not cover the full remaining balance.
