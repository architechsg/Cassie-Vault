# Basic Car Maintenance — Electric Vehicles (EV) (#AT22V)

EV-focused version of the hands-on car maintenance course. Covers maintenance practices specific to electric vehicles.

## Key Details
- **Duration:** 1 day (8 hours), 9am – 6pm
- **Class size:** Minimum 3 pax, maximum 10 pax per class

## Course Content


- **What you'll learn:** This Basic Electric Car Maintenance Course is conducted at a real-life Ubi car workshop, providing actual hands-on experience on a real car. At the end of the course, learners will be able to: LO1: Describe the functions and operations of internal and peripheral systems found in Class 3 vehicles EV/HEV.; LO2: Interpret fault codes and different warning indicators.; LO3: Conduct checks and inspections to verify the working conditions of various Class 3 EV/HEV vehicle parts.; LO4: Perform charging with the correct voltage charging plug.; LO5: Apply appropriate safety measures while handling EVs and HEVs.; LO6: Regulate WSH electrical hazards and use of PPE.; LO7: Perform the Isolation process using the LOTO.
- **Course modules:** Basic EV Maintenance Course Content This EV maintenance course provides a comprehensive introduction to the maintenance aspects of electric and hybrid vehicles, focusing on safety and basic upkeep. 1. Introduction to Electric Vehicles and Hybrid Electric Vehicles 2. Differences Between EVs and HEVs Types and functions of EV and HEV systems; Components and operational principles; High Voltage Safety Handling and WSH Electrical Hazard Awareness 3. Electrical Isolation Procedures for EVs and HEVs Personal protective equipment (PPE) for high-voltage work environments; Safety precautions and best practices; EV and High Voltage Vehicle Charging Modes & Systems 4. Types of Charging Systems Charging modes and connectors; Charging infrastructure and considerations; EV and High Voltage Vehicle Architecture, Components, and Systems
- **Who should attend:** Workers currently working in the vehicular repair and maintenance industry, such as: Self-sponsored individual learners who may be unemployed or may not be currently in the vehicular repair and maintenance industry, but are keen to embark on a career in this line; Junior/Assistant Mechanics; Workers for companies (in other sectors) that have a fleet of vehicles, who may be tasked to carry out basic maintenance of their vehicles; Supervisors and managers of the above staff; Those who have some working experience in the maintenance industry and wish to receive systematic training and WSQ certification for their skills.
- **Prerequisites:** At least 16 years old; Have a minimum primary school education; Have a basic knowledge of the Singapore Workplace Safety and Health requirements; Able to speak, listen, write, and read English with Numeracy skills at a minimum proficiency level of the Employability Skills Workforce Skills Qualification, i.e., Workplace Literacy and Numeracy (WPLN) Level 3; No prior maintenance/technical work experience required Attire Comfortable and practical clothing suitable for a workshop environment.; Closed-toe shoes are mandatory for safety reasons.
- **Assessment & certification:** Assessment Mode: Open-Book Summative; Assessment Methods and Duration: Practical Performance: 0.5 hour Written Assessment: 0.5 hour Participants who are assessed as Competent will receive a Statement of Attainment (SOA) issued by SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG)
