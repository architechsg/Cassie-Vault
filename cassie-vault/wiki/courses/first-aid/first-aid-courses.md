## Basic Cardiac Life Support + AED (BCLS+AED) (#AB61)
- **OLA module_id:** 2423
- **Duration:** 1 day (8 hours)
- **Funding:** No SSG subsidy (Non-WSQ).
- **Type:** Non-WSQ Certifiable
- **Note:** No TGS code.

- **What you'll learn:** This course aims to provide relevant knowledge and skills on CPR and the relief of foreign body airway obstruction in conscious and unconscious infant and adult casualties. This course will also provide you with the knowledge and skills on how to use an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) on an adult casualty. Recognizing and managing heart attack; Understanding the chain of survival; Performing cardiopulmonary resuscitation on an adult victim; Performing cardiopulmonary resuscitation on an infant victim; Use of an automated external defibrillator on an adult victim; Recognizing and managing an adult choking victim; Recognizing and managing an infant victim
- **Course modules:** During the course, learners will learn: Adult Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation; Adult Relief of Foreign Body Airway Obstruction; Child Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation; Child Relief of Foreign Body Airway Obstruction; Infant Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation; Infant Relief of Foreign Body Airway Obstruction; Application of Automated External Defibrillator (AED)
- **Who should attend:** The Basic Cardiac Life Support + AED (BCLS + AED) course is a mandatory training course for BCLS + AED instructors-trainers. They have to attain the BCLS + AED certificate before they are eligible for instructor-trainer. Examples: BCLS Course (CPR + AED) Singapore Medical & Healthcare Professionals (Dentist and Clinic Assistants) Childcare Professionals in learning Infant and Children resuscitation Individuals interested to become Basic Cardiac Life Support (BCLS) + AED Trainer
- **Prerequisites:** Able to speak, read and understand English effectively as our courses are conducted in English.; No history of underlying medical heart and lung conditions; Physically fit with no pre-existing medical conditions such as knee, spinal or joint injury.; Pregnant ladies are discouraged from undertaking the CPR practice due to safety reasons.
- **Assessment & certification:** Written Assessment; Case Study Assessment Participants who are assessed as Competent will receive a Statement of Attainment (SOA) issued by SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG).
