# Basic Peranakan Cuisines (#AT42)

WSQ course covering traditional Peranakan cooking methods and recipes.

## Key Details
- **Duration:** 3 days (24 hours), 9am – 6pm

## Course Content


- **What you'll learn:** Whether you’re a home cook or a curious beginner, our hands-on Peranakan cooking class will teach you authentic Nonya techniques so you can confidently recreate these traditional dishes and celebrate Singapore’s rich culinary heritage. By the end of this Peranakan cooking class, participants will be able to: Identify the cultural significance of Peranakan cuisine and recognize key traditional ingredients; Apply foundational techniques to prepare classic Nonya dishes such as Achar, Kueh Pie Tie, Popiah, Dry Mee Siam, Kapitan Chicken Curry, and Sugee Cake; Comply with essential food hygiene standards and maintain a safe cooking environment; Assemble appropriate tools and appliances, select quality ingredients, and portion them correctly; Follow instructions accurately, control time and temperature, and troubleshoot to achieve desired texture, consistency, and visual appeal;
- **Course modules:** This course will equip learners with the knowledge and skills to prepare an assortment of classic Nonya dishes that reflect Singapore’s rich Peranakan heritage. Learners will acquire this expertise through a combination of hands-on practical training and classroom learning: Peranakan Achar – Tangy pickled vegetables with aromatic spices; Kueh Pie Tee – Crispy pastry shells filled with savory vegetables; Nyonya Popiah – Fresh spring rolls with flavorful fillings; Dry Mee Siam – Spicy stir-fried vermicelli noodles; Kapitan Chicken Curr y – Rich, fragrant curry with traditional spices; Sugee Cake – Classic semolina cake with buttery texture Learners will also gain essential skills in ingredient selection, kitchen setup, food hygiene, and workstation management to ensure authentic and high-quality results.
- **Who should attend:** This course is ideal for: Perfect for culinary enthusiasts, home cooks, and aspiring chefs interested in authentic Peranakan cuisine; Suitable for individuals aiming to earn a WSQ certification in basic food preparation skills. Examples: Peranakan/Nyonya Cooking Course Culinary Enthusiasts – passionate about exploring heritage cuisines Home Cooks – eager to learn authentic Peranakan recipes Aspiring Chefs – looking to broaden their cooking skills Food Hobbyists – interested in cultural and traditional dishes Individuals Seeking WSQ Certification – for basic food preparation skills
- **Prerequisites:** Candidate must maintain proper dress code and adhere to all of the following pre-requisites when attending the class: Learners should be at least 18 years old.; Learners should have at least primary school or equivalent education.; Learners must be able to listen, speak, read, and write English at a level equivalent to Workplace Literacy – Level 4 and above.; Learners who do not meet the above requirements will be required to go through a short interview to verify their language and cognitive ability.
- **Assessment & certification:** Assessment for this course will be conducted through practical demonstrations and written evaluations, ensuring you’ve grasped both the theoretical knowledge and hands-on skills. Attendance requirement: min 75% attendance; Course completion requirements: Fulfil at least 75% minimum attendance and pass assessments – competent (Refer to mode of assessment for details); Participants who fulfill all requirements will receive a Statement of Attainment (SOA) issued by SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG). Validity The Statement of Attainment (SOA) issued by SkillsFuture Singapore is a lifetime certificate with no expiry date and does not require renewal.
