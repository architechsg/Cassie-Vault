# Environmental Cleaning Courses

All cleaning courses are provided by **Associates Consulting Pte Ltd** and are part of the Progressive Wage Model (PWM) for the Cleaning industry. Call `get_course_schedule` for live pricing and UTAP/PSEA/Mid-Career SFC top-up eligibility.

## WSH Level 1 — Workplace Safety and Health Practices (Cleaning) (#AB11)
- **OLA module_id:** 1802 | **TGS:** TGS-2023037590
- **Duration:** 1 day (7.5 hrs) | **Funding until:** 4 Sep 2027
- **Funding:** SFC eligible (WSQ)

- **What you'll learn:** This WSQ Workplace Safety and Health course, or Comply with Workplace Safety and Health Policies and Procedures, is developed to train cleaning professionals in applying WSH for the cleaning industry by providing them with occupational health and safety training. This allows them to perform cleaning services safely in accordance with workplace safety and health regulations and organisational procedures. This course is a module in WSQ Environmental Cleaning. At the end of this WSQ safety course, participants will be able to: State the importance of WSH.; Identify risks, hazards, and unsafe acts present in the work environment.; Participate in WSH practices within the scope of their own role and responsibilities, to prevent accidents or minimise risks.; Raise unsafe acts or conditions to supervisors according to company procedures..
- **Course modules:** This one-day WSH course provides a comprehensive introduction to essential workplace safety and health practices. Participants will learn about industry standards, risk control, and emergency response procedures within the cleaning sector. No. Module Details 1. Unit 1: Follow Safe Work Practices and Risk Control Procedures – Practice personal hygiene at the workplace. – Donning and doffing personal protective equipment is required for work. – Use workplace equipment and safety devices. – Recognise safety signs and instructions in the workplace.Follow workplace risk control measures. – Report workplace safety and health issues. 2. Unit 2: Participate in [Workplace Safety and Health Management] Activities – Recognise typical unsafe working conditions and work practices during work. – Perform correct work practices when conducting work.
- **Prerequisites:** Have basic knowledge of the work role and the corresponding responsibilities and limitations; Have at minimum a primary school education; Develop Writing, Reading, Listening and Speaking of English to a proficiency level equivalent to Workplace Literacy and Numeracy (WPLN) Level 2; Develop Numeracy skills to a proficiency level equivalent to Workplace Literacy and Numeracy (WPLN) Level 2; No prior cleaning work experience required.
- **Assessment & certification:** Practical Performance; Oral Questioning (Summative and Open Book) Participants who are assessed as Competent will receive a Statement of Attainment (SOA) issued by the SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG).

## WSH Level 3 — Workplace Safety and Health (Cleaning) (#AB31)
- **OLA module_id:** 2291 | **TGS:** TGS-2024041178
- **Duration:** 2 days (16 hrs) | **Funding until:** 2 Jan 2028
- **Funding:** SFC eligible (WSQ)

- **What you'll learn:** Safety and Health Practices Implementation (WSH Level 3) course is designed to equip supervisors and team leaders with the knowledge and skills to implement safe work practices and maintain workplace risk control measures. It focuses on compliance with WSH regulations and practical application in the environmental services industry.
- **Course modules:** Modules covered in the WSH Level 3 Course include: Module 1: Understand WSH Act and bizSAFE Framework; Module 2: Identify Workplace Hazards; Module 3: Conduct Risk Assessment; Module 4: Develop and Implement a Risk Management Plan; Module 5: Prepare Documentation for bizSAFE Level 3 Certification
- **Who should attend:** Supervisors and team leaders responsible for workplace safety in the environmental services sector. They are required to complete the WSQ Workplace Safety and Health Practices Implementation (WSH Level 3) course. This includes: Individuals supervising cleaning crews or maintenance teams in commercial, industrial, or public facilities.; Personnel overseeing operations in environmental services, such as waste management, pest control, or sanitation services.; Team leaders tasked with implementing WSH procedures and ensuring compliance with safety regulations at worksites..
- **Prerequisites:** Learners are encouraged to: Have basic knowledge of their own work role and the corresponding responsibilities and limitations; Have a minimum secondary school education; Demonstrate proficiency in Writing, Reading, Listening and Speaking of English at a level equivalent to Workplace Literacy and Numeracy (WPLN) Level 3; Develop Numeracy skills at a proficiency level equivalent to Workplace Literacy and Numeracy (WPLN) Level 3;
- **Assessment & certification:** Case Study (Summative and Open Book); Oral Questioning (Summative and Open Book); Role Play Upon successful completion of all assessments, participants will receive a bizSAFE Level 3 Recognition Certificate issued by the Workplace Safety and Health Council (WSHC). This certification validates the organization’s ability to implement a Risk Management Plan (RMP) in compliance with Singapore’s WSH Act and Risk Management Regulations. The bizSAFE Level 3 certificate is valid for 3 years. To maintain certification, companies must review and update their Risk Management Plan regularly and renew their bizSAFE status before expiry.

## Work Permit Holders
Work Permit (WP) holders are eligible to attend cleaning courses. SSG subsidies and SkillsFuture Credit do not apply — the **full fee** is payable. Upon passing, WP holders receive the same WSQ Statement of Attainment and can use it for NEA licensing requirements. On the day, WP holders should bring their FIN card and a Gmail account for e-attendance (SingPass is for SC/PR only).
