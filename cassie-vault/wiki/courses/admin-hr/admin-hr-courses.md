## Office Management Skills for Admin Professionals (#AB71)
- **OLA module_id:** 2457 | **TGS:** TGS-2025060579
- **Duration:** 1 day (8 hrs) | **Funding until:** 26 Nov 2027
- **Funding:** SFC eligible (WSQ)

- **What you'll learn:** ve professionals with essential skills to effectively engage and manage workplace stakeholders. Participants will learn to interpret stakeholder roles, analyse their impact on organisational functions, and apply practical communication techniques to assess needs and respond to queries professionally. Through a combination of structured instruction and applied practice, learners will also develop coordination strategies to align daily activities with stakeholder expectations. By the end of the course, participants will be better prepared to contribute proactively and confidently to stakeholder engagement and organisational success.
- **Course modules:** By the end of the lesson, the learners will be able to: LO1: Explain the roles different stakeholders play in the context of administrative and human resource work; LO2: Describe the functions and impact of key stakeholders on the organisation and HR/administrative functions; LO3: Apply communication techniques to assess stakeholder needs and interests; LO4: Respond effectively to stakeholder queries with clarity and professionalism; LO5: Coordinate daily activities with stakeholders to support operational flow
- **Who should attend:** Admin Executives, Office Managers, Executive Assistants, HR Assistants/Coordinators, and anyone coordinating cross-functional office operations. Examples: Office Management Skills for Admin Professionals) Admin Executive Office Manager Executive Assistant (EA) HR Assistants/Coordinators
- **Prerequisites:** 1 year of working experience in any sector; and; Workplace Literacy and Numeracy (WPLN) 4 or equivalent *Learners without the right WPLN or equivalent qualifications can be accepted if they have at least 3 years of relevant working experience in HR or HR-related roles, subject to the MR’s approval. Attire Comfortable and practical clothing suitable for a workshop environment.; Closed-toe shoes are mandatory for safety reasons.
- **Assessment & certification:** Written Assessment; Case Study Assessment Participants who are assessed as Competent will receive a Statement of Attainment (SOA) issued by SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG).

## Manage Workplace Health and Wellness (#AB72)
- **OLA module_id:** 2452 | **TGS:** TGS-2025060306
- **Duration:** 2 days (16 hrs)
- **Funding:** SFC eligible (WSQ). Funding period: contact staff to confirm.

- **What you'll learn:** Home / Courses / Manage Workplace Health and Wellness After attending this course, learners will appreciate the importance of health and wellness at the workplace as well as acquire the skills and knowledge required to review, improve, and implement health & wellness programmes at the workplace..
- **Course modules:** By the end of the lesson, the learners will be able to: LO1. Recognise new trends and technologies that promote healthy lifestyles •; LO2. Organise research into best practices and data-related legislative requirements in employee health and wellness management; LO3. Collect feedback from employees regarding existing and desired health and wellness programmes; LO4. Identify future programme focus areas based on the current state of health and wellness of different employee groups; LO5. Communicate health and wellness programmes to employees effectively to garner anticipation and buy-in; LO6. Perform planned employee health and wellness programmes implementation
